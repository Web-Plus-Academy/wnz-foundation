SW-CET • Common Intake Test

Saredufy-WebNexZ Common Entrance Test

A common screening assessment for WebNexZ Foundation Fellowship and Saredufy Launchpad programs — focused on aptitude, fundamentals, and learning readiness.

Why SW-CET?

SW-CET (Saredufy–WebNexZ Common Entrance Test) is designed as a unified intake and screening exam for multiple learning pathways.

The exam evaluates logical thinking, basic web fundamentals, and problem-solving ability — not prior expertise or advanced coding.

Eligibility

  • Students pursuing or completed undergraduate studies (any discipline)
  • Career aspirants and self-learners interested in web development
  • Basic computer literacy
  • Willingness to learn and follow structured guidance
  • Availability requirements may vary by program (Fellowship / Launchpad)

Exam Structure

  • Total Marks: 200
  • Duration: 30 min
  • Exam Mode: Online
  • Question Type: MCQs
  • Marking Scheme: +4 marks for each correct answer, −1 mark for each incorrect answer
  • Purpose of Exam: Common screening test for Fellowship & Launchpad intake

Section A: General Aptitude (GA)

Evaluates logical thinking, analytical ability, quantitative reasoning, and problem-solving skills.

120 Marks

Section B: Web Fundamentals

Tests basic computer awareness and beginner-level programming knowledge required for web development.

80 Marks

SW-CET Exam Syllabus

The syllabus is beginner-friendly and evaluates aptitude, logical thinking, and foundational web knowledge.

Section A: General Aptitude (GA)

  • Logical & analytical reasoning
  • Quantitative aptitude (basic arithmetic)
  • Pattern recognition & sequences
  • Problem-solving scenarios
  • Critical thinking & decision making

Section B: Web Fundamentals

  • Computer fundamentals & internet basics
  • Programming basics (logic, variables, conditions)
  • Understanding of algorithms & flow concepts
  • Basic awareness of web technologies
  • Logical problem-solving using simple code concepts

Evaluation Criteria

  • SW-CET exam performance
  • Section-wise eligibility thresholds
  • Application responses and intent clarity
  • Program-specific screening (where applicable)

SW-CET determines eligibility for different programs. Final selection criteria vary independently for Fellowship and Launchpad.

Important Guidelines

  • SW-CET can be taken only once per intake cycle
  • No malpractice or external assistance allowed
  • Results will be communicated via email
  • Program allocation depends on eligibility and seat availability

Scholarship & Program Allocation Policy

SW-CET (Saredufy–WebNexZ Common Entrance Test) is a common screening and eligibility assessment used to evaluate candidates for multiple learning programs. Scholarships and fellowships are awarded based on merit, eligibility criteria, and program-specific selection processes.

WebNexZ Foundation Fellowship (100% Scholarship)

  • Fully funded fellowship with limited seats
  • SW-CET is used for shortlisting eligible candidates only
  • Final selection includes additional screening such as interviews or tasks
  • Selection is based on merit, commitment, discipline, and availability
  • High SW-CET scores alone do not guarantee fellowship selection

Saredufy Launchpad (Merit-Based Scholarships)

  • SW-CET determines eligibility for Launchpad admission
  • Scholarships are offered in slabs based on SW-CET performance
  • Scholarship percentage may vary up to a maximum limit per intake
  • Eligible candidates may choose to join with or without scholarship
  • Admission is subject to seat availability and program guidelines

Candidates may be eligible for one or more programs based on SW-CET results. Participation in any program is optional, and final enrollment depends on acceptance of the respective program’s terms and expectations.

Note: Scholarship and fellowship decisions are made by the Academic Committee of Saredufy & WebNexZ Foundation. The committee reserves the right to modify selection criteria based on intake requirements and seat capacity.
