Fellowship Selection Process

Fellowship Entrance Exam

A screening assessment designed to evaluate intent, fundamentals, and readiness — not prior expertise.

Why an Entrance Exam?

The WebNexZ Foundation Fellowship is free but selective. The entrance exam ensures that participants are genuinely committed, capable of following structure, and ready to grow within a community-driven environment.

This exam is not meant to filter out beginners. It is designed to identify learners with the right mindset, discipline, and problem-solving approach.

Eligibility

  • Students pursuing or completed undergraduate studies (any discipline)
  • Early-stage developers or self-learners
  • Basic computer literacy
  • Ability to commit 2–3 hours daily during the fellowship
  • Willingness to contribute back to the community for 1 year

Exam Structure

Mode: Online (Objective + Short Answers)

Duration: 60 Minutes

Total Questions: 25–30

Difficulty Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Negative Marking: No

Entrance Exam Syllabus

The syllabus focuses on logical thinking, basic web awareness, and problem-solving.

1. Logical Thinking & Aptitude

  • Basic reasoning
  • Pattern recognition
  • Problem-solving approach
  • Simple algorithmic thinking

2. Programming Fundamentals

  • Variables & data types (conceptual)
  • Conditions & loops (logic-based)
  • Understanding pseudocode

3. Web Basics

  • What is the web?
  • Web Technology (awareness level)
  • What is Frontend?

4. Learning Mindset

  • Scenario-based questions
  • Discipline & consistency evaluation
  • Ethical and community behavior

Evaluation Criteria

  • Entrance exam score
  • Application responses
  • Consistency & clarity of intent
  • Community mindset

High scores alone do not guarantee selection. Candidates showing learning intent and discipline are prioritized.

Important Guidelines

  • The exam can be taken only once per intake cycle
  • No malpractice or external assistance allowed
  • Results will be communicated via email
  • Selected candidates proceed to the fellowship onboarding stage
