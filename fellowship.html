4-Month Intensive Program

WebNexZ Foundation Fellowship

A transformative 16-week journey from beginner to community leader, designed to build not just technical skills, but leadership qualities.

Apply for Fellowship

Who This Is For

  • Aspiring web developers eager to learn
  • Students looking for structured guidance
  • Self-learners seeking community support
  • Individuals committed to long-term growth

Who This Is NOT For

  • Those looking for quick certification
  • People unable to commit 2-3 hours daily
  • Anyone not interested in teaching others
  • Those expecting passive, video-only learning

Program Structure

A structured journey starting with an entrance exam, followed by a selective fellowship, and culminating in mentorship and leadership.

1

Stage 1

Entrance Assessment

An initial screening test to assess basic aptitude, logical thinking, learning intent, and commitment. This ensures that selected fellows are motivated and ready for the journey ahead.

2

Stage 2

Fellowship (Core Learning)

A 16-week fellowship covering HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React through structured weeks, hands-on tasks, peer learning, and continuous reviews.

3

Stage 3

Capstone Project

Fellows build a real-world, end-to-end project that demonstrates technical depth, problem-solving ability, and production-level thinking.

4

Stage 4

Mentorship & Leadership

Successful fellows transition into mentors, guiding junior batches, reviewing work, leading discussions, and contributing to the growth of the WebNexZ community.

What Fellows Get

Structured Learning Roadmap

Clear weekly milestones and objectives

Mentor Guidance

Personal mentorship from experienced developers

Leadership Opportunities

Grow into community leadership roles

Real Project Experience

Build portfolio-worthy projects

What Fellows Commit

The fellowship is free, but it requires genuine commitment. Here's what we expect from every fellow:

  • Complete daily tasks and assignments
  • Actively support and help peers
  • 1-year community contribution commitment
  • Attend weekly sync sessions

1-Year Commitment: This is an ethical, non-legal commitment to stay active in the community, mentor juniors, and contribute to the foundation's growth.
